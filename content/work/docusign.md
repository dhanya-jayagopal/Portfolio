I started working with the notifications team. Docusign's multi-channel notification system had grown organically over years, and the result was a tangle of inconsistent APIs, unpredictable delivery times, and a month-long onboarding process for any team that needed to send a notification.

I standardized the developer experience, built a self-service onboarding flow, and drove time-to-send from one month to three days. The scheduler platform — automated reminders for agreements, reports, and dashboards — followed from there, and led to a measurable reduction in missed signings.

Later I transitioned to the usage tracking team. Docusign was moving from a simple per-envelope pricing model to a user license and credits-based model. That sounds like a finance decision. It was actually an enormous platform engineering and product challenge.

Every feature needed to be tracked. Every customer needed a view into their consumption. Pricing, packaging, and reporting teams all had different definitions of what "usage" meant — and none of them talked to each other.

My job was to align them, define the data model, and ship the usage platform before the pricing transition landed.

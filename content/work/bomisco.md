Building a product from nothing is an exercise in radical prioritization. At Bomisco, I own the full product surface: roadmap, customer discovery, AI integration strategy, and day-to-day prioritization with engineering.

The core product is an end-to-end channel data management platform — helping companies manage the messy, duplicate-laden data that flows from distribution partners. The hardest part isn't the data. It's the handling, transforming and enriching the infinite combinations and rules to make this data insightful.

The partner gateway module handles the inbound data collection — a combination of structured APIs, portal integrations, email body, attachments, EDI.
The rules engine lets customers define their own enrichment and exceptions logic, without needing an engineer in the loop.
The data insights module surfaces trends and anomalies in the resolved dataset, so operations teams can act before problems compound.
We built AI-assisted entity resolution as an enhancement, using a combination of semantic matching, rules-based logic and LLM assistance. This helps figure out that "Acme Corp," "ACME Corporation," and "Acme Co." are all the same company.

I use Claude Code for rapid prototyping and Vercel V0 for UI iteration. The feedback loop between idea and working prototype is now measured in hours, not weeks.

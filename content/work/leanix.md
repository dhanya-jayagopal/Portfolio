I joined LeanIX as an Associate PM and grew with the Reference Catalogs product through three distinct phases.

In year one, the focus was building the Lifecycle Catalog, a comprehensive product metadata that gave IT teams visibility into software lifecycle status. This was the revenue-preservation play: customers who had real data about their software portfolios churned at a fraction of the rate. We protected 7% of ARR.

In year two, the catalog grew. Search, recommendation algorithms, and big data import capabilities all went through rapid iteration cycles. A 30% increase in user engagement came from removing friction, not adding features.

Year three brought two significant challenges simultaneously. First, the Cleanshelf acquisition: we had two months to integrate Cleanshelf's SaaS data into the catalog. We did it. Second, the SAP partnership: delivering Reference Architecture metadata to key customers through a partnership offering created new cross-sell opportunities.

The Tech Category Catalog, a standardized taxonomy for technical capabilities,shipped to 100% of customers by the end of my tenure. This helped build the product as a thought leadership tool.
